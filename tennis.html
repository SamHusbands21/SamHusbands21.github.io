How it works

A quick overview of the model and how value bets are identified. For full performance metrics and analysis, see the project paper →

Overall Elo

Each player carries a standard Elo rating (K=32) updated after every result. Unlike football, there is no home advantage — courts are neutral for both players.

Surface Elo

Three separate Elo pools are maintained for Hard, Clay, and Grass courts. Only the surface-specific rating is updated each match, capturing specialists like clay-court dominators whose overall Elo understates their surface strength.

Form & serve quality

Rolling win rates over the last 5 and 10 matches (overall and surface-specific). Rolling serve statistics — 1st serve percentage, 1st serve points won, and break-point save rate — all shift-rolled over the last 10 matches to prevent leakage.

Recovery & fatigue

Days of rest since the previous match (capped at 21 days) and the duration in minutes of that previous match. A player coming off a 3-hour five-setter two days ago is materially more fatigued than one who had a week's rest after a straight-sets win.

Model

An XGBoost classifier and a calibrated Random Forest trained on ATP matches 2005–2020 with walk-forward time-series cross-validation. Final probabilities average both calibrated outputs. Training data is randomly flipped 50/50 to prevent positional bias.

Value bets

A bet is flagged when model probability × Betfair decimal odds > 1.20. The Kelly fraction (capped at 25%) is shown as a suggested stake size. Recommendations are sourced from Betfair Exchange live odds.
