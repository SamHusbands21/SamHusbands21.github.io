How It Works
1

Poll Scraper

Scrapes all post-2024 Westminster polls from Wikipedia. Strips footnotes, normalises party names, deduplicates on pollster × date × sample size.

2

Kalman Aggregator

A random-walk Kalman filter smooths the raw polls into a latent support time series. Pollster house effects are estimated by EM iteration and removed before aggregation.

3

MRP Model

Mixed-effects logistic regression on BES 2024 microdata. Demographics and geography predict individual vote intent; poststratified against census cells per constituency.

4

ML Swing Model

XGBoost trained on 2017 and 2019 constituency swings (tested on 2024). Predicts how each seat diverges from the national swing based on local demographics.

5

Monte Carlo Seat Projections

10,000 draws from the Kalman posterior (multivariate normal, preserving cross-party correlations). FPTP applied per constituency each draw — seat distributions show genuine uncertainty.

Disclaimer: These projections are a personal research project and are not intended as political advice. Polling averages and seat models carry substantial uncertainty, especially between elections. Scotland and Wales are modelled with dedicated regional party adjustments (SNP calibrated within Scottish seats; Plaid Cymru shares derived directly from BES microdata for Welsh constituencies). Northern Ireland's 18 seats are not modelled — the NI party system is entirely separate from GB polling, so the 650-seat total used here should be read as the 632 GB seats only. A full NI treatment is planned for a future release.
